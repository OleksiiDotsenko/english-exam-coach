# Writing task anatomy — structure and register per task type

Original reference describing the expected shape of each task type. Facts
about formats only; no official content.

## Quick matrix

| Task | Words | Time | Register | Must have |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| IELTS Academic T1 report | ≥150 | 20 min | Neutral-formal | Overview sentence; grouped comparisons; no opinion |
| IELTS General T1 letter | ≥150 | 20 min | Matches recipient | Purpose in ¶1; all 3 bullets; fitting open/close |
| IELTS T2 essay | ≥250 | 40 min | Formal | Clear position; ¶ per idea; conclusion |
| TOEFL email | — | 7 min | Per scenario | All 3 given content points; email conventions (greeting, purpose, sign-off) |
| TOEFL academic discussion | ≥100 | 10 min | Natural-academic | Own contribution that advances the thread; reason + example |
| B1 email | ~100 | ~20 min | Friendly | Answers all 4 notes; opening/closing formulas |
| B1 article/story | ~100 | ~25 min | Informal-neutral | Title (article) / given first line respected (story) |
| B2 essay | 140–190 | ~40 min | Formal-neutral | Both given points + own point; conclusion |
| B2/C1 article | in range | — | Engaging | Title, hook, direct address to reader |
| B2/C1 report / C1 proposal | in range | — | Formal, impersonal | Section headings; recommendation(s) at the end; proposal argues for future action |
| B2/C1 review | in range | — | Semi-formal | What is reviewed + evaluation + recommendation |

## Anatomy notes by family

### Data/report family (IELTS Academic T1)
Introduction (paraphrase the source line) → overview (2–3 dominant trends,
no numbers) → 2 body paragraphs of grouped, quantified comparisons. Penalise
in feedback: missing overview, mechanical listing of every figure, opinion
or speculation, copied prompt wording.

### Letter/email family (IELTS General T1, B1 email, B2/C1 email or letter)
Purpose stated early; one paragraph per content point; register consistent
with the named recipient (friend vs. manager vs. stranger); conventional
opening/closing pairs. Penalise: register drift, unaddressed bullet points.

### Essay family (IELTS T2, B2/C1/C2 essays, TOEFL discussion post)
Position visible from the introduction and never contradicted; each body
paragraph = one idea + support (example, consequence, concession);
conclusion restates without copying. C1/C2 input-based essays additionally
require covering the given points in the writer's own words and evaluating,
not just reporting them.

### Workplace-document family (reports, proposals)
Headings expected; impersonal constructions favoured; findings → conclusion
→ recommendation flow. A proposal differs from a report in tense and intent:
it argues for something not yet done.

### Review/article family
Reader-facing voice, a hook, evaluative language with concrete detail;
review ends in a clear recommendation; article benefits from a question or
direct address.

## Feedback priority order

When choosing the 3–5 fixes, prioritise what moves the estimate most:
1. Task fulfilment (missing content point, wrong genre, word count) —
   caps the score regardless of language quality.
2. Organisation (paragraphing, progression, cohesion overuse/underuse).
3. Lexical resource (repetition, imprecision, collocation errors).
4. Grammatical range (only simple forms) and accuracy (systematic errors).
5. Style/register last, unless the genre demands it (letters, articles).
