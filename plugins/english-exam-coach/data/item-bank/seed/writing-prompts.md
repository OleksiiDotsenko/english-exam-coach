# Seed writing prompts (original examples)

All items below are original, written for this plugin as style/format
references for generating new prompts. They are NOT taken from any official
exam or preparation material. Match structure, register and constraints —
never reuse a seed verbatim in a drill; generate a fresh item in its shape.

## IELTS Academic — Task 1 (report, ≥150 words, 20 min)

> The table below shows the percentage of households in three countries that
> owned a bicycle, a car, or both in 2005 and 2025.
>
> | | Bicycle only | Car only | Both |
> |---|---|---|---|
> | Country A (2005 → 2025) | 34% → 18% | 22% → 41% | 15% → 24% |
> | Country B (2005 → 2025) | 12% → 15% | 55% → 48% | 20% → 27% |
> | Country C (2005 → 2025) | 41% → 44% | 9% → 13% | 8% → 19% |
>
> Summarise the information by selecting and reporting the main features, and
> make comparisons where relevant.

*(When a chart cannot be rendered, present the data as a table like this.)*

## IELTS General Training — Task 1 (letter, ≥150 words, 20 min)

> You recently borrowed a piece of equipment from a neighbour, and it stopped
> working while you were using it. Write a letter to your neighbour. In your
> letter: explain what happened, apologise for the situation, and say what
> you intend to do about it. Begin your letter "Dear ...,".

## IELTS / general — Task 2 (essay, ≥250 words, 40 min)

> Some people believe that public libraries should focus on digital services
> rather than physical books. To what extent do you agree or disagree? Give
> reasons for your answer and include any relevant examples from your own
> knowledge or experience.

## TOEFL iBT (2026 format) — Write an Email (7 min, 3 content points)

> You borrowed a projector from the campus media office for a student club
> event, and one of its cables was damaged during the evening. Write an
> email to the media office. In your email:
> - explain what happened,
> - say what you have already done about it, and
> - ask what you should do next.

## TOEFL iBT (2026 format) — Writing for an Academic Discussion (10 min, ≥100 words)

> **Professor:** This week we discussed how cities manage growing traffic.
> Some cities invest in wider roads; others restrict cars in the centre.
> Before class, I'd like you to weigh in: which approach do you think serves
> a growing city better, and why?
> **Student A (Maya):** Wider roads just invite more cars. Restricting the
> centre pushes people to transit, which scales better.
> **Student B (Tomas):** Restrictions punish people who have no transit
> option. Build capacity first, restrict later.
> Write a post responding to the professor's question. Contribute your own
> ideas to the discussion.

## B2-level essay (140–190 words)

> In your English class you have been talking about shopping habits. Write an
> essay giving your opinion on the statement: "Small local shops cannot
> survive alongside large online retailers." Include points on **price** and
> **community**, and add one further point of your own.

## C1-level proposal (220–260 words)

> Your college has received a grant to improve student wellbeing and has
> invited proposals. Write a proposal for the college principal outlining one
> or two initiatives, explaining how they would improve wellbeing and why
> they would be a good use of the grant.

## C2-level essay (240–280 words, summarise and evaluate two texts)

> Read the two texts below. Write an essay summarising and evaluating the key
> points from both texts, using your own words throughout.
>
> **Text 1 — The case for slow news.** Rolling coverage rewards speed over
> understanding. A daily digest, edited with hindsight, gives readers
> proportion: what mattered, what merely happened. Slower rhythms may be the
> only cure for a public exhausted by alerts. *(≈60 words)*
>
> **Text 2 — Speed is a public service.** During emergencies, minutes matter,
> and instant reporting has saved lives. The answer to shallow coverage is
> better editing, not artificial delay; readers can choose their own pace,
> and it is patronising to choose it for them. *(≈60 words)*
