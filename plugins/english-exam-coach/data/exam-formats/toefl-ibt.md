# TOEFL iBT — format facts (redesigned test, from 21 January 2026)

Factual description of the test format in use since January 21, 2026. No
official test content. Figures marked "approx." are consistent
prep-community reporting where ETS publishes no exact number; confirm
details with the exam provider.

**Structure:** 4 sections in fixed order **Reading → Listening → Writing →
Speaking**. Working time ≈ 85–90 min (allow ~2 h for the appointment). No
breaks. Note-taking allowed throughout. Reading and Listening are
**multistage adaptive**: everyone gets a first "router" stage, then an
easier or harder second stage based on performance; item counts and times
vary slightly per person.

**Scale (since Jan 2026):** each section scored in **bands 1–6 in half-band
steps**; overall = average of the four sections rounded to the nearest half
band. Score reports through ~January 2028 also show a comparable legacy
0–120 score. Scores post ~3 days after the test; validity 2 years.

**Official CEFR alignment (overall and per section):**

| Band | 6.0 | 5.0–5.5 | 4.0–4.5 | 3.0–3.5 | 2.0–2.5 | 1.0–1.5 |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| CEFR | C2 | C1 | B2 | B1 | A2 | A1 |

**Legacy 0–120 concordance (overall, official):** 6.0 ≈ 114+, 5.5 ≈ 107+,
5.0 ≈ 95+, 4.5 ≈ 86+, 4.0 ≈ 72+, 3.5 ≈ 58+, 3.0 ≈ 44+.

## Reading — ~30 min · up to 50 items · adaptive

| Task type | Mechanics |
|---|---|
| Complete the Words | C-test cloze: short paragraphs where about every other word has its second half deleted; type the missing letters (~10 gaps per paragraph) |
| Read in Daily Life | Short practical texts (emails, notices, schedules, posts, ~15–150 words); 2–3 multiple-choice questions each |
| Read an Academic Passage | Short academic passage (~200 words); ~5 multiple-choice questions (main idea, detail, vocabulary in context, inference) |

The old long-passage format (2 × ~700 words, 10 questions each) is gone.

## Listening — ~29 min · up to 47 items · adaptive

| Task type | Mechanics |
|---|---|
| Listen and Choose a Response | Hear one short prompt (plays once, ~5 s, no transcript); pick the most natural reply from 4 options |
| Listen to a Conversation | Short campus-life dialogue, 2 speakers; ~2 questions |
| Listen to an Announcement | Single-speaker campus/academic announcement (~40–85 words); ~2 questions |
| Listen to an Academic Talk | Short lecture (~100–250 words); ~4 questions |

Audio plays once; notes allowed.

## Writing — 23 min · 12 items · linear

| Task | Items | Time | Mechanics |
|---|---|---|---|
| Build a Sentence | 10 | ~6–7 min total | Arrange scrambled words/phrases into a grammatical sentence, often a reply in a short exchange |
| Write an Email | 1 | 7 min | Email for an academic/social situation; 3 required content points are given |
| Write for an Academic Discussion | 1 | 10 min | Read a professor's forum question + two student posts; contribute your own post with a supported opinion |

The old integrated reading–listening essay is gone; there are no integrated
tasks anywhere in the redesigned test.

## Speaking — ~8 min · 11 items · linear

| Task | Items | Mechanics |
|---|---|---|
| Listen and Repeat | 7 | Hear short sentences of rising difficulty (once each); repeat each exactly (~8–12 s window, approx.) |
| Take an Interview | 4 | Simulated interview on academic/campus topics; 45 s per answer, no preparation time |

All four old speaking tasks (independent opinion + three integrated tasks)
are gone.

## Delivery

Test centres and the TOEFL iBT Home Edition worldwide, both using this
format. The Paper Edition was discontinued in January 2024. One legacy
carve-out: **TOEFL iBT Australia** — a separate test-centre administration
that keeps the pre-2026 format and 0–30/0–120 scoring, currently the only
TOEFL accepted for Australian visa purposes. (For that legacy format:
sections were Reading/Listening/Speaking/Writing at 0–30 each, ~2 h total.)
