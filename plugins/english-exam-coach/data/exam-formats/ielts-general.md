# IELTS General Training — format facts

Factual description of the test format only. No official test content.
Figures are for orientation; confirm details with the exam provider.

**Scale:** bands 0–9 in half-band steps, per section and overall.

**Indicative CEFR alignment:** 4.0–5.0 ≈ B1 · 5.5–6.5 ≈ B2 · 7.0–8.0 ≈ C1 ·
8.5–9.0 ≈ C2.

## Listening — identical to IELTS Academic

~30 min + 2 min review, 4 parts, 40 questions. See `ielts-academic.md` for
the part breakdown, question types, and delivery notes (computer-delivered
since mid-2026; One Skill Retake available).

## Reading — 60 min · 3 sections · 40 questions

Texts are everyday rather than academic, increasing in difficulty:

| Section | Sources | Focus |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Notices, advertisements, timetables | Social survival: factual detail |
| 2 | Job descriptions, contracts, staff manuals | Workplace survival |
| 3 | One longer text of general interest | Detailed comprehension |

Question types mirror the Academic module (matching, T/F/NG, completion,
multiple choice, short answer). Band conversion is slightly more demanding
than Academic for the same raw score.

## Writing — 60 min · 2 tasks

| Task | Minimum words | Suggested time | Task |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 150 | 20 min | Letter (formal, semi-formal or informal) requesting information or explaining a situation |
| 2 | 250 | 40 min | Essay on a topic of general interest, slightly less abstract than Academic Task 2 |

Task 2 carries twice the weight. Same four public criteria names as Academic.

## Speaking — identical to IELTS Academic

11–14 min, 3 parts (interview → long turn with cue card → discussion). Same
four public criteria names.
