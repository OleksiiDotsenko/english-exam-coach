# CEFR B1 exam — format facts

Factual description of the format of the Cambridge English B1-level
qualification, marketed as "B1 Preliminary" (name used nominatively). No
official test content. Figures are for orientation; confirm details with
the exam provider.

**Scale:** Cambridge English Scale. B1 pass ≈ 140–159; 160–170 shows B2
ability (Grade A); 120–139 reports at A2. Each paper contributes 25%.

**Delivery:** paper and digital versions share the same format; Speaking is
always face-to-face with an examiner.

## Reading — 45 min · 6 parts · 32 questions

| Part | Items | Task type |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | 5 | Short real-world texts (signs, messages): 3-option multiple choice |
| 2 | 5 | Matching five people to eight short texts |
| 3 | 5 | Longer text: 4-option multiple choice |
| 4 | 5 | Gapped text: choose sentences to fill gaps |
| 5 | 6 | Multiple-choice cloze (vocabulary focus) |
| 6 | 6 | Open cloze (one word per gap, grammar focus) |

## Writing — 45 min · 2 parts

| Part | Words | Task |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | ~100 | Email reply to given notes (compulsory) |
| 2 | ~100 | Choice: article **or** story |

Assessed on content, communicative achievement, organisation and language
(criterion names are public).

## Listening — 30 min (incl. 6 min transfer on paper) · 4 parts · 25 questions

Recordings are heard twice.

| Part | Items | Task type |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | 7 | Short recordings: choose the correct picture |
| 2 | 6 | Short dialogues: 3-option multiple choice |
| 3 | 6 | Monologue: gap fill (write words/numbers) |
| 4 | 6 | Interview: 3-option multiple choice |

## Speaking — 10–12 min per pair (15–17 in groups of three) · 4 parts

| Part | Time | Format |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | 2–3 min | Interview: personal questions |
| 2 | 2–3 min | Extended turn: describe a colour photograph (~1 min each) |
| 3 | 2–3 min | Collaborative discussion based on a picture scenario |
| 4 | 3 min | General discussion linked to Part 3 topic |
