# CEFR C1 exam — format facts

Factual description of the format of the Cambridge English C1-level
qualification, marketed as "C1 Advanced" (name used nominatively). No
official test content. Figures are for orientation; confirm details with
the exam provider.

**Scale:** Cambridge English Scale. C1 ≈ 180–199; 200–210 shows C2 ability
(Grade A); 160–179 reports at B2. Papers: Reading & Use of English 40%,
Writing / Listening / Speaking 20% each.

**Delivery:** paper and digital versions share the same format; Speaking is
always face-to-face with an examiner.

## Reading & Use of English — 90 min · 8 parts · 56 questions

| Part | Items | Task type |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | 8 | Multiple-choice cloze (vocabulary, collocation, idiom) |
| 2 | 8 | Open cloze (one word per gap, grammar) |
| 3 | 8 | Word formation |
| 4 | 6 | Key word transformations (3–6 words including the key word) |
| 5 | 6 | Long text: 4-option multiple choice |
| 6 | 4 | Cross-text multiple matching: compare four short texts' opinions |
| 7 | 6 | Gapped text: reinsert removed paragraphs |
| 8 | 10 | Multiple matching: locate information |

## Writing — 90 min · 2 parts · 220–260 words each

| Part | Task |
|---|---|
| 1 | Essay: summarise and evaluate two of three given points from input notes (compulsory) |
| 2 | Choice: email/letter, proposal, report or review |

Assessed on content, communicative achievement, organisation and language
(criterion names are public).

## Listening — ~40 min · 4 parts · 30 questions

Recordings are heard twice.

| Part | Items | Task type |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | 6 | Three short dialogues: 3-option multiple choice |
| 2 | 8 | Monologue: sentence completion |
| 3 | 6 | Interview/discussion: 4-option multiple choice |
| 4 | 10 | Five themed monologues: double multiple matching |

## Speaking — ~15 min · 4 parts · in pairs

| Part | Time | Format |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | 2 min | Interview: personal questions |
| 2 | 4 min | Long turn: compare two of three photographs (1 min) + 30 s partner question |
| 3 | 4 min | Collaborative task: discuss written prompts, then reach a decision (1 min) |
| 4 | 5 min | Discussion of questions related to Part 3 |
