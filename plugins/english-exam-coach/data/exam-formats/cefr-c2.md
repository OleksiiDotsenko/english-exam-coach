# CEFR C2 exam — format facts

Factual description of the format of the Cambridge English C2-level
qualification, marketed as "C2 Proficiency" (name used nominatively). No
official test content. Figures are for orientation; confirm details with
the exam provider.

**Scale:** Cambridge English Scale. C2 ≈ 200–230; 180–199 reports at C1.
Papers: Reading & Use of English 40%, Writing / Listening / Speaking 20% each.

**Delivery:** paper and digital versions share the same format; Speaking is
always face-to-face with an examiner.

## Reading & Use of English — 90 min · 7 parts · 53 questions

| Part | Items | Task type |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | 8 | Multiple-choice cloze (idiom, collocation, shades of meaning) |
| 2 | 8 | Open cloze (one word per gap) |
| 3 | 8 | Word formation |
| 4 | 6 | Key word transformations (3–8 words including the key word) |
| 5 | 6 | Long text: 4-option multiple choice |
| 6 | 7 | Gapped text: reinsert removed paragraphs |
| 7 | 10 | Multiple matching: locate information |

## Writing — 90 min · 2 parts

| Part | Words | Task |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | 240–280 | Essay: summarise and evaluate the key points of two ~100-word input texts (compulsory) |
| 2 | 280–320 | Choice: article, letter, report or review (a set-text option may appear) |

Assessed on content, communicative achievement, organisation and language
(criterion names are public).

## Listening — ~40 min · 4 parts · 30 questions

Recordings are heard twice.

| Part | Items | Task type |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | 6 | Three short extracts: 3-option multiple choice |
| 2 | 9 | Monologue: sentence completion |
| 3 | 5 | Interview/discussion: 4-option multiple choice |
| 4 | 10 | Five themed monologues: double multiple matching |

## Speaking — ~16 min · 3 parts · in pairs

| Part | Time | Format |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | 2 min | Interview: personal and opinion questions |
| 2 | 4 min | Collaborative task: discuss pictures, speculate and reach a decision |
| 3 | 10 min | Long turn + discussion: each candidate speaks 2 min from a question card, then group discussion |
