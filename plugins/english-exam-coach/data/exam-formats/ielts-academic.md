# IELTS Academic — format facts

Factual description of the test format only (task types, counts, timing,
scales). No official test content. Figures are for orientation; formats
occasionally change — confirm details with the exam provider before test day.

**Scale:** bands 0–9 in half-band steps, per section and overall (average of
the four sections, rounded to the nearest half band).

**Indicative CEFR alignment:** 4.0–5.0 ≈ B1 · 5.5–6.5 ≈ B2 · 7.0–8.0 ≈ C1 ·
8.5–9.0 ≈ C2.

**Delivery (since mid-2026):** computer-delivered at test centres — paper-based
sittings were retired in most markets in June 2026. A "Writing on Paper"
option (computer test, handwritten Writing) exists in selected markets. A One
Skill Retake lets you retake exactly one section within 60 days of the
original test.

## Listening — ~30 min + 2 min review · 4 parts · 40 questions

| Part | Recording | Typical focus |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Conversation, 2 speakers, everyday context | Names, numbers, form/note completion |
| 2 | Monologue, everyday context | Map/plan labelling, matching |
| 3 | Conversation, up to 4 speakers, study context | Opinion, agreement, multiple choice |
| 4 | Academic monologue (lecture) | Note/summary completion |

Question types across parts: form/note/table/sentence completion, multiple
choice, matching, map/plan/diagram labelling, short answer. Recordings are
heard once only. Each question = 1 mark; raw score converts to a band.

## Reading — 60 min · 3 passages · 40 questions

Three long academic texts (journals, books, magazines, newspapers),
increasing in difficulty. Question types: matching headings, True/False/Not
Given, Yes/No/Not Given, matching information/features/sentence endings,
sentence completion, summary/note/table/flow-chart completion, diagram
labelling, multiple choice, short answer. Each question = 1 mark; no extra
transfer time.

## Writing — 60 min · 2 tasks

| Task | Minimum words | Suggested time | Task |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 150 | 20 min | Describe visual information (graph, table, chart, diagram, process, map) in your own words |
| 2 | 250 | 40 min | Discursive essay responding to a point of view, argument or problem |

Task 2 carries twice the weight of Task 1. Assessment criteria (names are
public): Task Achievement (T1) / Task Response (T2), Coherence & Cohesion,
Lexical Resource, Grammatical Range & Accuracy.

## Speaking — 11–14 min · 3 parts · face-to-face interview

| Part | Time | Format |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | 4–5 min | Introduction and interview: familiar topics (home, work, studies, interests) |
| 2 | 3–4 min | Long turn: task card, 1 min preparation, speak 1–2 min, follow-up questions |
| 3 | 4–5 min | Discussion: abstract questions linked to the Part 2 topic |

Assessment criteria (names are public): Fluency & Coherence, Lexical
Resource, Grammatical Range & Accuracy, Pronunciation.
