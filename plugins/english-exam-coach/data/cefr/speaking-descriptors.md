# CEFR speaking descriptors (paraphrased)

Paraphrased in original wording from the public CEFR framework's qualitative
aspects of spoken language (Council of Europe). Not an official mark scheme.
Assess along five aspects: range, accuracy, fluency, interaction, coherence.

## B1

- **Range:** enough language to describe experiences, events, hopes and plans,
  with some hesitation and circumlocution on less familiar topics.
- **Accuracy:** reasonably accurate use of a repertoire of frequent routines
  and patterns tied to predictable situations.
- **Fluency:** keeps going comprehensibly, though pauses for grammatical and
  lexical planning and repair are noticeable, especially in longer turns.
- **Interaction:** can start, maintain and close simple face-to-face
  conversation on familiar topics; repeats back to confirm understanding.
- **Coherence:** links discrete, simple elements into a connected, linear
  sequence of points.

## B2

- **Range:** sufficient language to give clear descriptions and viewpoints on
  most general topics, without conspicuous searching for words, using some
  complex sentence forms.
- **Accuracy:** relatively high grammatical control; mistakes do not lead to
  misunderstanding and are often self-corrected.
- **Fluency:** produces stretches of speech at a fairly even tempo; few
  noticeably long pauses despite some hesitation over patterns and expressions.
- **Interaction:** initiates discourse, takes turns appropriately, and helps
  the discussion along by confirming comprehension and inviting others in.
- **Coherence:** a limited number of cohesive devices link utterances into
  clear, connected discourse, though longer contributions may be "jumpy".

## C1

- **Range:** broad command of language allowing precise formulation, emphasis
  and elimination of ambiguity across complex topics.
- **Accuracy:** consistently high accuracy; errors are rare, hard to spot and
  usually corrected in passing.
- **Fluency:** expresses ideas fluently and almost effortlessly; only a
  conceptually difficult subject slows the natural flow.
- **Interaction:** selects appropriate phrases to get and keep the floor and
  relates own contributions skilfully to other speakers'.
- **Coherence:** clear, smoothly flowing, well-structured speech showing
  controlled use of organisational patterns and connectors.

## C2

- **Range:** great flexibility in reformulating ideas in differing linguistic
  forms to convey finer shades of meaning, give emphasis and differentiate;
  strong command of idiomatic and colloquial expression.
- **Accuracy:** consistent control of complex grammar even while attention is
  elsewhere (e.g. on planning or on others' reactions).
- **Fluency:** long, natural, effortless flow; hesitates only to find the
  precisely right word or example.
- **Interaction:** interacts with ease and skill, picking up non-verbal and
  intonational cues; weaves own contribution into joint discourse naturally.
- **Coherence:** coherent and cohesive discourse making full and appropriate
  use of a wide variety of organisational patterns.

## Pronunciation note

CEFR treats phonological control separately: at B1 pronunciation is clearly
intelligible despite a noticeable accent; by C1–C2 stress, rhythm and
intonation are used flexibly to convey meaning precisely. Never penalise an
accent itself — only features that impede intelligibility.
