# CEFR reading descriptors (paraphrased)

Paraphrased in original wording from the public CEFR framework (Council of
Europe). Not an official mark scheme. Use to set text difficulty when
generating passages and to interpret drill results.

## B1

- **Overall:** reads straightforward factual texts on familiar topics with a
  satisfactory level of understanding.
- **Detail:** finds and understands relevant information in everyday material
  (letters, brochures, short articles); follows clearly written instructions.
- **Inference:** identifies main conclusions in clearly signalled argumentative
  text; deduces some unknown words from context on familiar topics.
- **Typical texts:** everyday narratives, simple news items, descriptions of
  events and feelings.

## B2

- **Overall:** reads with a large degree of independence, adapting speed and
  style of reading to purpose; has a broad active reading vocabulary but may
  struggle with low-frequency idioms.
- **Detail:** quickly scans long texts to locate relevant details; understands
  articles and reports on contemporary problems where writers take positions.
- **Inference:** recognises the line of argument, distinguishes main points
  from supporting detail, and understands explicitly signalled stance.
- **Typical texts:** press articles, reviews, reports, contemporary prose.

## C1

- **Overall:** understands long, demanding texts in detail, including
  specialised articles outside own field (occasionally rereading difficult
  sections).
- **Detail:** grasps finer points, implied attitudes and opinions as well as
  stated ones.
- **Inference:** follows complex argumentation, appreciates style and register
  shifts, and understands idiomatic expression and colloquialisms.
- **Typical texts:** academic and professional writing, literary prose,
  complex instructions.

## C2

- **Overall:** understands and interprets critically virtually all forms of
  written language, including abstract, structurally complex, or highly
  colloquial writing.
- **Detail:** appreciates subtle distinctions of style and implicit meaning;
  recognises irony, understatement and the writer's unstated purposes.
- **Inference:** understands texts whose point emerges only from accumulated
  implication rather than explicit statement.
- **Typical texts:** any — manuals, specialised articles, literary works.

## Listening note

The same progression applies to listening: B1 follows the main points of
clear standard speech on familiar matters; B2 follows extended speech and
complex argument on reasonably familiar topics; C1 follows extended speech
even when poorly structured and when relationships are only implied; C2
understands any variety of spoken language at natural speed.
