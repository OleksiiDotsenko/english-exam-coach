# CEFR writing descriptors (paraphrased)

Paraphrased in original wording from the public CEFR framework (Council of
Europe). Not an official mark scheme of any exam board. Use these to anchor
feedback and level estimates for written work.

## B1

- **Overall:** produces connected text on familiar or personally interesting
  topics; can narrate, describe and give simple reasons for opinions.
- **Range:** enough everyday language to get the message across, with visible
  circumlocution around harder ideas; sentence patterns are simple and
  repetitive.
- **Accuracy:** reasonable control of frequent patterns; errors appear when
  attempting complexity but the main point stays clear.
- **Organisation:** links a series of shorter elements into a linear sequence
  with basic connectors (and, but, because, then).

## B2

- **Overall:** writes clear, detailed text on a wide range of subjects; can
  argue for or against a viewpoint, weighing advantages and disadvantages, and
  synthesise information from more than one source.
- **Range:** sufficient vocabulary to vary formulation and avoid repetition;
  some complex sentence forms used, occasionally awkwardly.
- **Accuracy:** good grammatical control; occasional slips or non-systematic
  errors that do not cause misunderstanding, often self-corrected.
- **Organisation:** clear paragraphing; a limited but effective set of cohesive
  devices makes the line of argument easy to follow.

## C1

- **Overall:** writes clear, well-structured text on complex subjects,
  expanding and supporting points of view with relevant examples and rounding
  off with an appropriate conclusion.
- **Range:** broad lexical repertoire including idiomatic and colloquial
  expression; register and tone adjusted to purpose and reader.
- **Accuracy:** consistently high grammatical accuracy; errors are rare and
  hard to spot.
- **Organisation:** controlled use of a variety of organisational patterns and
  a wide range of connectors; paragraphs develop single ideas smoothly.

## C2

- **Overall:** writes clear, smoothly flowing, complex text in an appropriate
  and effective style, with a logical structure that helps the reader find
  significant points; can summarise and critique demanding source material.
- **Range:** very broad and precise repertoire; conveys finer shades of meaning
  and uses idiomatic language naturally, with awareness of connotation.
- **Accuracy:** maintains consistent grammatical control of complex language,
  even while attention is elsewhere; errors are exceptional.
- **Organisation:** creates coherent, cohesive text making full and natural use
  of organisational patterns and cohesive devices.
