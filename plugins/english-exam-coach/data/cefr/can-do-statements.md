# CEFR can-do statements (paraphrased self-assessment grid)

Paraphrased in original wording from the public Council of Europe
self-assessment grid. Use for quick level diagnostics ("which of these can
you comfortably do?") and to explain to a learner what the next level means.

## B1 — independent user (threshold)

- **Listening:** follow the main points of clear standard speech on familiar
  matters (work, school, leisure), including slow, clear radio/TV items.
- **Reading:** understand texts built from everyday or job-related language;
  understand descriptions of events and feelings in personal letters.
- **Spoken interaction:** handle most situations that come up while
  travelling; join unprepared conversations on familiar topics.
- **Spoken production:** connect phrases to narrate experiences, dreams and
  ambitions; briefly justify opinions and explain plans; retell a story.
- **Writing:** produce simple connected text on familiar topics; write
  personal letters describing experiences and impressions.

## B2 — independent user (vantage)

- **Listening:** follow extended talks and lectures with reasonably familiar
  subject matter; understand most news and current-affairs broadcasts.
- **Reading:** read articles and reports where writers adopt stances;
  understand contemporary literary prose.
- **Spoken interaction:** interact with native-level speakers fluently enough
  for regular exchange without strain for either side; argue a position.
- **Spoken production:** give clear, detailed presentations on many subjects;
  explain a viewpoint giving advantages and disadvantages of options.
- **Writing:** write clear, detailed text on a wide range of subjects — an
  essay or report passing on information or arguing for or against a view.

## C1 — proficient user (effective operational proficiency)

- **Listening:** follow extended speech even when it is not clearly
  structured and relationships are only implied; understand most media and
  films without excessive effort.
- **Reading:** understand long, complex factual and literary texts,
  appreciating style; understand specialised articles outside own field.
- **Spoken interaction:** express ideas fluently and spontaneously without
  much obvious word-searching; use language flexibly for social and
  professional purposes.
- **Spoken production:** present clear, detailed descriptions of complex
  subjects, integrating sub-themes and rounding off appropriately.
- **Writing:** produce clear, well-structured expository text on complex
  subjects in a style appropriate to the intended reader.

## C2 — proficient user (mastery)

- **Listening:** understand any kind of spoken language, live or broadcast,
  at natural native speed (given brief accent familiarisation).
- **Reading:** read virtually all forms of written language with ease,
  including abstract or structurally complex texts.
- **Spoken interaction:** take part effortlessly in any conversation,
  handling idiom and colloquialism; reformulate around any difficulty so
  smoothly others barely notice.
- **Spoken production:** present a clear, smoothly flowing argument in a
  style fitting the context, structured to help listeners catch the key
  points.
- **Writing:** write clear, smoothly flowing text in an appropriate style —
  complex letters, reports or articles; summarise and review professional or
  literary works.
